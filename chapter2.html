Chapter 2: Words That Wound While Claiming God

When harm is delivered through the language of faith

Some of the deepest wounds do not come from silence. They come from words spoken with certainty, authority, and the claim that God stands behind them.

The difficulty is not only what was said. The difficulty is who those words were attributed to. When God’s name is attached to harm, confusion follows.

Real Situation One

A person opens up about identity, struggle, or internal conflict. The response comes quickly and firmly.

“God is not pleased with this.” “You need to change.” “This is why things are not working in your life.”

No space is given to process. No care is taken to understand. The conversation closes as quickly as it opened.

The result is not clarity. The result is distance. Distance from people. Distance from self. Distance from God.

What Happened

Words were used to control direction rather than guide it. Authority replaced understanding. Certainty replaced discernment.

The name of God was used to reinforce the message, making it difficult to question or process what was said.

Real Situation Two

A setting where teachings are delivered with intensity. Every message is framed as absolute truth. Disagreement is treated as rebellion.

Statements are repeated often: “This is what God says.” “There is no other way.”

Over time, questions become internalized. Fear replaces curiosity. Compliance replaces understanding.

What Happened

Language created pressure instead of clarity. The environment demanded agreement rather than growth.

Words were presented as truth, yet the result was fear rather than life.

The Internal Conflict

When harmful words are connected to God, a person is left with limited options.

Accept the words and lose connection with self, or reject the words and feel separated from God.

That tension is not created by Jesus. That tension is created by misrepresented authority.

God’s voice does not remove dignity. God’s voice does not create confusion about worth. God’s voice brings clarity, even when correction is present.
Scripture Framing

Jeremiah 23:16 speaks directly to voices that claim God while speaking from their own perspective.

Ezekiel 33:31 shows how words can sound correct externally, while the internal result remains unchanged.

The presence of God is not confirmed by tone, volume, or certainty. The result reveals the source.

Separating God from the Experience

Not every word spoken in the name of God comes from Him. Not every strong statement reflects truth.

Jesus, who is God revealed, consistently moved toward people with clarity, presence, and purpose.

Correction from Jesus leads somewhere. Harm leaves a person without direction.

Final Observation

Words that wound while claiming God often leave lasting impact. The issue is not only the moment. The issue is what follows.

Rebuilding trust requires separating the voice of Jesus from the voices that misrepresented Him.
