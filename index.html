Words That Sound Good but Lead Away from Truth

A structured examination of voice, truth, and discernment

Not every word that comforts carries truth. Not every voice that feels right is from God.

This book walks through real-life situations where words, environments, and voices appear aligned, yet lead away from clarity. Each chapter builds toward one outcome: the ability to recognize the voice of Jesus with confidence.

Many, including those within the LGBTQIA+ community, have experienced moments where identity, faith, and belonging became sources of confusion rather than clarity. Some encountered rejection in the name of God. Others encountered acceptance without direction. This work does not dismiss those experiences. It brings clarity to them, separating the voice of Jesus from everything that has misrepresented Him.

This is not a devotional and not a collection of abstract ideas. This is a structured, step-by-step examination of how words, voices, and environments shape understanding.

The focus is not only on identifying what is false, but on clearly recognizing what is true.

The voice of Jesus remains consistent, clear, and grounded. This book exists to make that voice recognizable again.

What This Book Covers
Words that sound good but lack truth
Words that wound while claiming God
Discernment between human voices and the voice of Jesus
Identity, internal conflict, and real-life decisions
Rebuilding trust and standing in truth without approval
